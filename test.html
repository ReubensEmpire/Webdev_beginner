James webb telescope

NASA's astrophysics research process involves: * Data collection: Gathering data from space-based and ground-based telescopes, such as Hubble, James Webb Space Telescope, and various ground-based observatories.

*

modeling:

Using computer simulations to create models of celestial objects and phenomena, helping scientists understand their behavior and properties.

*

Hypothesis testing:

Comparing theoretical models with observational data to test the validity of hypotheses and refine our understanding of the universe.

*

Publication and peer review:

Publishing research papers in scientific journals and undergoing rigorous peer review to ensure the quality and accuracy of the findings.

*

Collaboration:

Working closely with other space agencies, universities, and research institutions around the world to share knowledge and resources, fostering a collaborative approach to scientific discovery.
